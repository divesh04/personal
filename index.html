About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), working with Prof. Sergio Pellegrino on the development of next-generation space solar power satellites. I completed my undergraduate studies at the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), where I worked with Kurien Issac on the design of a small deployable truss prototype for reflector antenna.

My research spans multiple aspects of deployable structures, including design, simulation, and experimental testing. My current work involves design of an attitude control device for Caltech's Space Solar Power Project (SSPP) , where I design experiments to perform slew maneuvers of a flexible-body using a momentum exchange device.

Research

Conventionally, attitude control design for spacecraft is performed using Hardware In Loop Simulations (HILS). These models are deterministic and prone to detachment from real world physics. As a result, designers often update algorithms by trial and error from prior on-orbit experience. This demands several spacecraft of similar nature to be flown before converging to a robust design. For an extremely large spacecraft such as SSPP with proposed 60 m size, this methodology is not feasible and requires alternative strategies.

Targeted research areas

  • Control Moment Gyroscope (CMG) design and prototype
  • Control algrothm for trajectory tracking in attitude
  • Flexible structure - CMG interaction
  • Data driven identification and control (ongoing)
